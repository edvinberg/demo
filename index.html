ONE TO FIVE-FORMAT

  • FEEDBACK

"I show you what i have done, you tell me whats wrong and how to fix it"

Setup

  1. HI! Who are you and what you have done (10 sec)
  2. WOW! Show what it does (< 2 min)
  3. AHA! what is it? / what have you done? (< 3 min)
  4. ? Ideas & questions (< 4 min)
  5. GOODBYE! Finish by offering a channel for feedback (5 sec)

Rules

  • Keep time
  • Do not talk about stuff, show stuff
  • Show the main functionalities
  • Use most part of the demo for feedback and questions, let the audience deside what to talk about
  • Roslings law - keep it stupid and simple. Keep things out of the demo

Example presentation

  1. - Hi, my name is Kalle and i have made a flying rocket
  2. Kalle picks up the rocket and pushes the button that makes it fly
    Wow!
  3. This is how the rocket works...
    The engine is...
  4. -Any questions?
    Nobody says anything.
    Kalle talks about the future of the project and what the next big thing is
    The audience asks what tech is used when building this
    Kalle is happy they brought it up, he likes talking about this
  5. Kalle finishes by showing his email and asks for feedback and questions.

Content tips

Avoid slides and tabs, the demo is a process in showing your thing

  1. Who are you and what you have done

    Short presentation of you and your tean

    Oneliner about what you have done

  2. Show what it does

    Show the main functionality.

    Dont talk about how or why

    TIPS:

    • Show a use case
    • Live coding

  3. what is it? / what have you done?

    How did you do this?

    How did you design this?

    Who will use this?

    TIPS:

    • If you thing solves a problem, show the problem - dont talk about it
    • If you want to talk about why this is a a good idea - dont, show it

  4. Ideas & questions

    Ask for it

    Discussion and questions decides what to focus on/p>

    TIPS:

    • Be prepared of tougher technical questions, and how to handle if you dont know the answer
    • Be prepared for questions about next steps

  5. Finish by thanking the audience and give a channel for feedback

    Show how they can contact you
